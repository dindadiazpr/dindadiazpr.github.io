A teacher who still gets excited about gold stars.

I work with preschool, kindergarten, and elementary learners, and I have a real soft spot for the little ones. At this age English is not a subject, it is a game we play with songs, colors, and stories. I meet every child where they are, including two wonderful young learners with ADHD, and I find the way that helps each one shine. I would love to bring that energy to an international school.

Inside my classroom

Phonics & Letters

We sound out letters with actions, flashcards, silly voices, and playful learning apps until the alphabet feels like a friend. Little hands trace, build, and spell their very first English words.

Story Time

Big pictures, funny voices, and "what happens next?" Stories and gentle screen time grow vocabulary and imagination at once, and they are every class's favorite moment.

Songs & Play

Hello songs, counting chants, movement games, and hands-on projects like growing our own bean sprouts. When children sing, move, and discover, English slips in without anyone noticing they are learning.

Show & Tell

Each child brings something they love and tells us about it in English. It builds confidence, listening, and the joy of being heard.
